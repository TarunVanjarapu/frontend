MotoGP

What is MotoGP

The MotoGP, or Grand Prix Motorcycle Racing, is the premier motorcycle championship in the world. It is the oldest motorsport championship in the world, with the first-ever annual competition being held in 1949. The governing body for MotoGP is the Fédération Internationale de Motocyclisme (FIM).

Races: It is a non-stop race from start to finish without any pit stops. Each race lasts between 40 and 45 minutes over a distance of between 100 and 130 km, depending on the circuit. Engine Capacity

Earlier labelled to the “500cc” category only, in 2002, this oldest motorcycle championship underwent a change and increased the engine capacity to 990cc. Again in 2007, the rules were altered, limiting the engine capacity to 800cc, which was again changed to 1000cc in 2012. The championship sees multiple races around the world with a season that goes from March until November. The championship is run on Points basis. Points are won by each Rider (finishing 1st, 2nd, 3rd till the 15th position) and each Team (Team Championship) and Constructor (Constructors’ Championship)




Overview

History
The first official motorcycle race was the Isle of Man TT in 1907, and the first world championship for road motorcycles was organized in 1949 by the Fédération Internationale de Motocyclisme (FIM). MotoGP replaced the previous premier class in 2002.

Rules
The MotoGP World Championship consists of 21 Grand Prix races, each lasting between 40 and 45 minutes. The first 15 riders receive points at the finish line.

Machines
MotoGP machines have around 300 horsepower, weigh 157 kilograms, and can reach speeds of more than 350 kilometers per hour.

Charity
MotoGP's official charity works with Riders for Health to support programs in Lesotho, Nigeria, Malawi, and The Gambia.

Events
A typical race weekend includes three 45-minute free practice sessions, with the times set in those sessions counting towards qualifying.
